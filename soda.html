Soda & Swine UCSD Case Study



Overview

The UC San Diego campus hosts a fast-casual dining experience exclusive to San Diego: Soda and Swine. Soda and Swine UCSD aims to provide a space that transports its patrons away from the workload brought upon by academic or professional work; a home away from home. The restaurant flaunts its old-timey, brassy, yet contemporary aesthetics that complement the modern Italian-inspired food selection. The establishment also stands as one of the few places on campus that provides a quality selection of craft beer and drafts. But with all the neat specialties up its sleeve, the restaurant falls behind on one feature that many other restaurants have already mastered: a website. Soda and Swine UCSD has a very minimal website that leaves potential patrons wondering about the types of selection and services offered by the restaurant. This lack of presentation can be the crucial turning point for a potential customer picking between Soda and Swine UCSD and competing restaurants.

Problem Statement

Soda and Swine UCSD needs a way to give potential patrons a complete view into the food selection and restaurant information to facilitate their decision in picking a restaurant.

Needfinding

To gain a sense of what customers wanted from the new Soda and Swine UCSD website, my group and I set out to interview a pool of people whom we thought represented the average customer base. Note: Before this stage, my group and I had already sat down with the General Manager at Soda and Swine UCSD so we already had an idea of who to interview for a good representation of customers. Our interviews would consist of persona questions (occupation, age), website-usage questions, and questions regarding familiarity with Soda and Swine. We selected people who fit the following categories: students, staff & faculty, and non-affiliates. The students, both undergraduates and graduates, represent the largest group of the customer base. Staff & faculty represent that second largest with the manager telling us of regulars that would go the restaurant after work or during lunchtime for their usual meal. The non-affiliates category contains customers who are neither students nor staff members but have taken a trip to the university campus to dine at the restaurant.

User Personas

Using the three categories and the responses we received from our interviews, we created three user personas that we believed to encapsulate the general needs and goals for these types of people. These personas would help us justify the design and functionality choices when presenting to clients and stakeholders.


Competitive Analysis

No tenacious application development would be complete without a thorough look at the current market trends. My team had several discussions to select the most relevant competitors whose websites to audit. We believed these competitors shared the same types of users so it was easy to audit where the competing websites fell short of meeting user needs. For a competitive audit, we reviewed websites from the following businesses found in close proximity to Soda and Swine UCSD: BJ’s Restaurant, Ballast Point Brewing Company, Taproom, Eureka!, and Dirty Birds.

Low-Fidelity Prototypes

This project was meant to grow/change an existing product so there was no need for hand-drawn sketches of novel layouts. Instead, these well-done and hand-drawn sketches also acted as our first prototype that we would use as a guide and as a first impression of our suggestions onto an existing UI.

Mid-Fidelity Prototypes

The mobile homepage was created with both a banner image and an image carousel because of indecision but also as a topic of discussion for user testing. The hamburger icon revealed access to the entire site architecture.

The food menu page, arguably the most important page on the site, put all the cards on the table. Every item was immediately available but the menu could be traversed with a drop down section submenu.

The drink menu was laid out nearly exactly as the food menu. This highlights the ability to click and expand any image which is doable on both the drink and food pages.

The events and services pages were also single-page sections accessible via hamburger icon from anywhere on the site.

The contact page holds all the location info and, ideally, an interactive Google Map.

Final Note

In the end, my group had created a fully interactive Figma prototype of the website we set out to design for a real client - Soda & Swine UCSD. As per our problem statement, we were aiming to create a website to inform and persuade potential patrons. Additionally, the client wanted a website that would increase weekend sales and draw more attention towards the beer selection. We did the research, jumped from iteration to iteration, and received invaluable user feedback. The final prototype is as close as we could have come to a real product with goals and constraints. The final test would have been turning the prototype into a working site and deploying it to measure its effectiveness in increasing sales. But in this case, the journey mattered more than the destination.
